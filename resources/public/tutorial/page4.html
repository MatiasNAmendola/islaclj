I bet that caught you by surprise, didn't it? Don't fear! Clojure simply has a built in Rational type. We can prove that we've got a rational by running (type (/ 10 3)). Rationals are more concise and precise than floating point numbers. However, we can force Clojure to do floating point division by just making one of our numbers floating point.

Type (/ 10 3.0) to continue.
